Theorem 1.   Given a number c >= 1, there exists a J-unitary operator \hat{V}, such that:   (a) r(\hat{V})= r(\hat{V}^{-1})= c ;   (b) S(c^{-1}\hat{V})=S(c^{-1}\hat{V}^{-1})   =S(c^{-1}\hat{V}^{*-1}) = S(c^{-1}\hat{V}^*)={0}   (c) there exist maximal strictly positive and strictly negative   \hat{V}^{\pm 1}-{invariant subspaces}   L_{+}, L_{-}, such that they are mutually J-orthogonal and   L_{+} + L_{-} is dense in the space.   (d_1) if L_1 is non-zero \hat{V}-invariant subspace, then r(\hat{V}|L_1)=r(\hat{V})   (d_2) if L_2 is non-zero \hat{V}^{-1}-invariant subspace, then r(\hat{V}^{-1}|L_2)=r(\hat{V}^{-1}) .
