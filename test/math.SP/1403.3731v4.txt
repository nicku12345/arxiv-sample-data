For an arbitrary nonempty, open set \Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^n, n \in \mathbb{N}, of finite (Euclidean) volume, we consider the minimally defined higher-order Laplacian (- \Delta)^m\big|_{C_0^{\infty}(\Omega)}, m \in \mathbb{N}, and its Krein--von Neumann extension A_{K,\Omega,m} in L^2(\Omega). With N(\lambda,A_{K,\Omega,m}), \lambda > 0, denoting the eigenvalue counting function corresponding to the strictly positive eigenvalues of A_{K,\Omega,m}, we derive the bound  N(\lambda,A_{K,\Omega,m}) \leq (2 \pi)^{-n} v_n |\Omega| \{1 + [2m/(2m+n)]\}^{n/(2m)} \lambda^{n/(2m)}, \quad \lambda > 0,  where v_n := \pi^{n/2}/\Gamma((n+2)/2) denotes the (Euclidean) volume of the unit ball in \mathbb{R}^n.   The proof relies on variational considerations and exploits the fundamental link between the Krein--von Neumann extension and an underlying (abstract) buckling problem.
