We consider a weighted Berezin transform:  B_{\alpha} : L^{\infty} (\mathbb{B}^n) \to \ \mathcal{B},\quad \alpha>-1, defined, for f \in L^{\infty} \left( \mathbb{B}^n \right) and z \in \mathbb{B}^n, by (B_\alpha f) (z) = c_\alpha \int_{\mathbb{B}^n} \frac{\left( 1-|z|^2 \right)^{n+1}}{|1 - \langle z, w \rangle|^{2n+2}} f(w) \left( 1-|w|^2 \right)^\alpha \ d v(w), where c_{\alpha} = \frac{\Gamma(\alpha+n+1)}{\Gamma(\alpha+1)\pi^n} , v is the Lebesque measure and \mathcal{B} is a Bloch-type space. We prove that B_{\alpha} is bounded iff \alpha>0 and give the exact semi-norm of  B_\alpha for 0\leq\alpha \leq 2n+3.
