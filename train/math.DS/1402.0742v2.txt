We present:   1) a mixing Z ^ 2-action with the following asymmetry of multiple mixing property: for some commuting measure-preserving transformations S, T and a sequence n_j  \lim_{j\to \infty}\mu(A\bigcap S^{-n_j}A\bigcap T^{-n_j}A)=\mu(A)^3 for all measurable sets A, but there is A_0, \mu(A_0)=\frac 1 2, such that  \lim_{j\to \infty}\mu(A_0\bigcap S^{n_j}A_0\bigcap T^{n_j}A_0)=0;   2) Z -actions with the asymmetry of the partial multiple mixing and the partial multiple rigidity:  \lim_{j\to \infty}\mu(A\bigcap T^{k_j}A\bigcap T^{m_j}A)= \frac23 \mu(A)^3+\frac13\mu(A),  \lim_{j\to \infty}\mu(A\bigcap T^{-k_j}A\bigcap T^{-m_j}A)= \mu(A)^2;   3) infinite transformations T such that for all A, \mu(A)<\infty, \lim_{j\to \infty}\mu(A\bigcap T^{k_j}A\bigcap T^{m_j}A)= \frac13\mu(A) and \lim_{j\to \infty}\mu(A\bigcap T^{-k_j}A\bigcap T^{-m_j}A)=0.
