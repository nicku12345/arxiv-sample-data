We first show that the subgroup of the abelian real group \mathbb{R} generated by the coordinates of a point in x = (x_1,\dots,x_n)\in\mathbb{R}^n completely classifies the \mathsf{GL}(n,\mathbb Z)-orbit of x. This yields a short proof of J.S.Dani's theorem: the \mathsf{GL}(n,\mathbb Z)-orbit of x\in\mathbb{R}^n is dense iff x_i/x_j\in \mathbb{R} \setminus \mathbb Q for some i,j=1,\dots,n. We then classify \mathsf{GL}(n,\mathbb Z)-orbits of rational affine subspaces F of \mathbb{R}^n. We prove that the dimension of F together with the volume of a special parallelotope associated to F yields a complete classifier of the \mathsf{GL}(n,\mathbb Z)-orbit of F.
