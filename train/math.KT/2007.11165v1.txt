For a central perfect extension of groups A \rightarrowtail G \twoheadrightarrow Q, we study the maps H_3(A,\mathbb{Z}) \to H_3(G, \mathbb{Z}) and H_3(G, \mathbb{Z}) \to H_3(Q, \mathbb{Z}) provided that A\subseteq G'. First we show that the image of H_3(A, \mathbb{Z})\to H_3(G, \mathbb{Z})/\rho_\ast(A\otimes_\mathbb{Z} H_2(G, \mathbb{Z})) is 2-torsion where \rho: A \times G \to G is the usual product map. When BQ^+ is an H-space, we also study the kernel of the surjective homomorphism H_3(G, \mathbb{Z}) \to H_3(Q, \mathbb{Z}).
