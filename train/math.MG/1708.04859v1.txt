We improve Marstrand's projection theorem onto lines in \mathbb{R}^{3}. Let \gamma \colon [0,2\pi) \to S^{2} be the curve defined by \gamma(\theta) = \tfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(\cos \theta, \sin \theta,1), and let \rho_{\theta} \colon \mathbb{R}^{3} \to \mathrm{span} \,\gamma(\theta) be the orthogonal projection. We prove that if K \subset \mathbb{R}^{3} is a Borel set, then \mathrm{dim}_{\mathrm{H}} \rho_{\theta}(K) = \min\{\mathrm{dim}_{\mathrm{H}} K,1\} for almost every \theta \in [0,2\pi), where \mathrm{dim}_{\mathrm{H}} denotes the Hausdorff dimension. For 0 \leq t < \mathrm{dim}_{\mathrm{H}} K, we also prove a bound for the Hausdorff dimension of those parameters \theta \in [0,2\pi) with \mathrm{dim}_{\mathrm{H}} \rho_{\theta}(K) \leq t < \mathrm{dim}_{\mathrm{H}} K.
