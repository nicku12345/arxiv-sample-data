Let \mathcal{H} be a complex Hilbert space, \mathcal{B(H)} be the algebra of all bounded linear operators on \mathcal{H} and \mathcal{A} \subseteq \mathcal{B(H)} be a von Neumann algebra without central summands of type I_1. For arbitrary elements A, B\in \mathcal{A}, one can define their \ast-Jordan product in the sense of A\diamond B = AB+BA^\ast. Let p_n(x_1,x_2,\cdots,x_n) be the polynomial defined by n indeterminates x_1, \cdots, x_n and their \ast-Jordan products. In this article, it is shown that a mapping \delta: \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{B(H)} satisfies the condition  \delta(p_n(A_1, A_2,\cdots, A_n))=\sum_{k=1}^n p_n(A_1,\cdots, A_{k-1}, \delta(A_k), A_{k+1},\cdots, A_n)  for all A_1, A_2,\cdots, A_n \in \mathcal{A} if and only if \delta is an additive \ast-derivation.
