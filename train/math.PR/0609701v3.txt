We give some approximations of the local time process (L_t^x)_{t\geqslant 0} at level x of the real Brownian motion (X_t). We prove that  \frac{2}{\epsilon}\int_0^{t} X_{(u+\epsilon)\wedge t}^+ \indi_{\{X_u \leqslant 0\}} du + \frac{2}{\epsilon}\int_0^{t} X_{(u+\epsilon) \wedge t}^- \indi_{\{X_u>0\}} du and \frac{4}{\epsilon}\int_0^{t} X_u^- \indi_{\{X_{(u+\epsilon) \wedge t} > 0\}} du converge in the ucp sense to L_t^0, as \epsilon \to 0. We show that  \frac{1}{\epsilon}\int_0^t (\indi_{\{x<X_{s+\epsilon}\}} - \indi_{\{x<X_{s}\}}) (X_{s+\epsilon}-X_{s})ds goes to L_t^x in L^2(\Omega) as \epsilon \to 0, and that the rate of convergence is of order \epsilon^\alpha, for any \alpha < {1/4}.
