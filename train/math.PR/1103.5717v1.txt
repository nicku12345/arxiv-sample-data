Let B_s be a three dimensional Brownian motion and \omega(dx) be an independent Poisson field on \mathbb{R}^3. It is proved that for any t>0, conditionally on \omega(\cdot),   \label{*} \mathbb{E}_0 \exp\{\theta \int_0^t \bar{V}(B_s) ds\} \ < \infty \ a.s. & \text{if} \theta< 1/16, \medskip   = \infty \ a.s. & \text{if} \theta> 1/16, where \bar{V}(x) is the renormalized Poisson potential    \bar{V}(x)=\int_{\mathbb{R}^3} \frac{1}{| x-y |^2} \big[\omega(dy)-dy\big].  Then the long term behavior of the quenched exponential moment \eqref{*} is determined for \theta \in (0, 1/16) in the form of integral tests.   This paper exhibits and builds upon the interrelation between the exponential moment \eqref{*} and the celebrated Hardy's inequality  \int_{\mathbb{R}^3} \frac{f^2(x)}{| x |^2} dx \le 4 \|\nabla f\|_2^2, 2in f \in W^{1,2}(\mathbb{R}^3).
