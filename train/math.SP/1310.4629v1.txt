We prove the spectral instability of the complex cubic oscillator -\frac{d^2}{dx^2}+ix^3+i\alpha x for non-negative values of the parameter \alpha, by getting the exponential growth rate of \|\Pi_n(\alpha)\|, where \Pi_n(\alpha) is the spectral projection associated with the n-th eigenvalue of the operator. More precisely, we show that for all non-negative \alpha \[   \lim\limits_{n\to+\infty}\frac{1}{n}\log\|\Pi_n(\alpha)\| = \frac{\pi}{\sqrt{3}}. \]
