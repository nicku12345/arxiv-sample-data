For a given bounded domain \Omega\subset {\Bbb R}^n with C^1-smooth boundary, we prove the P\'olya conjecture for the Neumann eigenvalues. In other words, we prove that \begin{eqnarray*} \mu_{k+1}\le \frac{(2\pi)^2k^{2/n}}{(\omega_n \cdot \mbox{vol}\, (\Omega))^{2/n}} \quad \;\; \mbox{for all} \;\; k=0,1,2,3,\cdots,\end{eqnarray*} where \mu_k is the k-th Neumann eigenvalue of the Laplacian for \Omega.
