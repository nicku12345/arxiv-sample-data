Let M_n^{(k)} denote the kth largest maximum of a sample (X_1,X_2,...,X_n) from parent X with continuous distribution. Assume there exist normalizing constants a_n>0, b_n\in \mathbb{R} and a nondegenerate distribution G such that a_n^{-1}(M_n^{(1)}-b_n)\stackrel{w}{\to}G. Then for fixed k\in \mathbb{N}, the almost sure convergence of \[\frac{1}{D_N}\sum_{n=k}^Nd_n\mathbb{I}\{M_n^{(1)}\le a_nx_1+b_n,M_n^{(2)}\le a_nx_2+b_n,...,M_n^{(k)}\le a_nx_k+b_n\}\] is derived if the positive weight sequence (d_n) with D_N=\sum_{n=1}^Nd_n satisfies conditions provided by H\"{o}rmann.
